Why are you here?

What possessed you to show up on a Saturday to talk about fermented food?
Fermentation can ...
  • make food more nutritious, even disease-fighting
  • preserve food easily
  • make food taste better.
  • make new kinds of food. Many common foods were originally fermented.

The process of fermentation

Fermentation means micro-organisms are consuming or transforming one thing into another. To ferment, you need three things:
  • Something to ferment. Milk, cabbage, flour, meat, others.
  • A micro-organism. These come from the air, or from a starter, which might simply be a bit of leftover from the last batch.
  • The proper environment. This means the proper temperature, salinity, pH, oxygen availability, etc.

Sourdough

Sourdough

Sourdough is easy. Mix flour with water. Wait.
  • Use the same kind of flour all the time
  • Feed it regularly
  • Use non-chlorinated water where possible
  • Keep it warm

Lacto-Fermentation

Lacto-Fermentation

Some bacteria produce lactic acid, which is an excellent preservative. These are called lactobacilli. They're found everywhere, but are especially common in raw milk and yogurt, and are used to ferment pickles, sauerkraut, and many other vegetables. They're more salt tolerant than many putrefying bacteria.

Vegetables

Fermenting Vegetables

The basic process for most vegetables is to clean them, mash them into a jar, add salt and any necessary water, and optionally, whey. Let it sit a while, and transfer to cool, dark storage.

This works for beets, cabbage, beans, kale, carrots, peppers, garlic, and many others. Softer vegetables are more difficult.

It fermented. Now what?

It fermented. Now what?

  • Trust your senses. Food that looks or smells bad probably is.
  • Try a little, and pay attention. If it burns or makes you dizzy, stop eating.
  • Spots of low-growing white mold are called Kahm yeast, and are generally not a problem.

Tips and Tricks

Tips and Tricks

  • Keep things clean. This is more important the longer you'll be storing an item.
  • Use non-chlorinated water. Chlorine is there to kill fermenting organisms.
  • Use the right amount of salt.
  • Use whey with more sugary ferments. It may make your results mushy.
  • Keep ferments separate, or they'll cross-contaminate.
  • Finding a reliable source of raw milk will change your life.

The next level

The next level

Fermented beverages are some of our favorites. They have similar health benefits, and can replace many unhealthy habits. These include kefir, ginger ale, and kombucha.

Kombucha

Originally from Russia, kombucha is a fermented, sweetened tea. The fermentation removes much of the caffeine, carbonates and acidulates the liquid, and produces several beneficial compounds.

Black tea with refined sugar works best; herbal teas can slow or stop the fermentation, and less refined sugars contribute off-flavors.
